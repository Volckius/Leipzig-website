St. Nicholas Church and the square
The St. Nicholas Church (Nikolaikirche) is one of the major churches of central Leipzig, Germany. Construction started in Romanesque style in 1165, but in the 16th century, the church was turned into a Gothic hall church. Baroque elements like the tower were added in the 18th century. In the 18th century, several works by Johann Sebastian Bach, who was as Thomaskantor the music director of Thomaskirche and Nikolaikirche from 1723 to 1750, premiered here. The Neoclassical interior dates to the late 18th century. The church rose to national fame in 1989 with the Monday Demonstrations when it became the centre of peaceful revolt against communist rule. By capacity, it is one of the largest churches in Saxony.
Zeitgeschichtliches Forum Leipzig (Contemporary History Forum Leipzig)
The Contemporary History Forum is a museum in Leipzig that deals with the history of the division of Germany , everyday life in the communist dictatorship of the GDR and the reunification process, as well as the challenges that the reunified Germany faces in the 21st century. The museum is a location of the House of History of the Federal Republic of Germany Foundation in Bonn , supported by the Federal Government Commissioner for Culture and Media . Uta Bretschneider has been the director since April 2020 . She succeeded Jürgen Reiche (September 2015 to January 2020). Rainer Eckert was the founding director from 1999 to 2015. The exhibition area covers 2000 square meters; Admission is free.
Culinary Specialties
Leipzig and Saxony, in eastern Germany, boast a rich culinary heritage beyond the ubiquitous Dresden Stollen and marzipan treats. A few of the most popular culinary specialties include the Leipziger Allerlei - an iconic vegetable stew features peas, asparagus, carrots, morels, and crayfish, often served with bread dumplings and Gose - A sour and refreshing wheat beer unique to Leipzig, traditionally fermented with lactic acid bacteria and seasoned with coriander.
Music
Leipzig echoes with centuries of musical genius. It began in 1212 with the founding of the St. Thomas Boys Choir, one of the oldest choirs in the world. Soon after, Johann Sebastian Bach arrived, composing masterpieces and raising the city's musical prestige. Felix Mendelssohn followed, transforming the Gewandhaus Orchestra into a global powerhouse. The Schumanns, Wagner, and Mahler found inspiration within its walls, solidifying Leipzig's reputation as a musical sanctuary. Even today, tradition meets innovation, with renowned opera productions, vibrant festivals, and a thriving contemporary scene ensuring Leipzig's melody continues to resonate.
Theatre and Opera
The Leipzig Opera (Oper Leipzig) is an opera house and opera company located at the Augustusplatz and the Inner City Ring Road at its east side in Leipzig's district Mitte, Germany. Performances of opera in Leipzig trace back to Singspiel performances beginning in the year 1693. The composer of many early operas at the first opera house, the Oper am Brühl, was Telemann. He was director of the house from 1703 to 1705. Construction of the modern opera house began in 1956. The theater was inaugurated on 8 October 1960 with a performance of Wagner's Die Meistersinger von Nürnberg.
