Old Town Hall & Leipzig Markt
The market square, together with the Old Town Hall, represents the center of Leipzig's city center and is the location of numerous events and markets. The Old Town Hall with the City History Museum is also located directly on the market square. Throughout the year you will find a wide variety of markets and events here: Easter market, wine festival, the Leipzig Market Music, the market days and, last but not least, of course the Christmas market. The weekly market attracts visitors twice a week with fresh regional products. A variety of shopping centers and shopping opportunities around the market reinforce its function as the hub of the city. On our first day in Leipzig our mentor took us here to get a tour of Leipzig to see what the city has to offer.
Statue of Mephistopheles & Faust
Sculpture of Mephistopheles bewitching the students in the scene "Auerbachs Keller" from Faust, at the entrance of what is today the restaurant Auerbachs Keller in Leipzig. Auerbachs Keller is the second oldest restaurant in Leipzig, Germany. Already one of the city's most important wine bars by the 16th century, it owes its worldwide reputation to Goethe's play Faust as the first place Mephistopheles takes Faust on their travels.
Alte Börse (Old Trading Exchange)
The Old Trading Exchange is Leipzig's oldest merchant meeting building and the city's oldest baroque building. Above the two-armed staircase, two winged putti hold the magnificent Leipzig city coat of arms . While the upper floor was used as a stock exchange hall , the ground floor was rented to merchants. The facades of the free-standing building are evenly decorated on all sides with tall rectangular windows and, above them, low transverse rectangular windows. The Old Trading Exchange combines elements of the Dutch and Italian Baroque, making it a very special piece of Leipzig architecture. The picture showcases the Goethe statue in front of the trading exchange.
St. Thomas' Church (Thomaskirche)
The St. Thomas Church is a Lutheran church in Leipzig, Germany, located at the western part of the inner city ring road in Leipzig's central district. Martin Luther preached in the church in 1539. It is associated with several well-known composers, especially Johann Sebastian Bach, who was its Thomaskantor (music director) from 1723 until his death in 1750. The church holds his remains. Although rebuilt over the centuries and damaged by Allied incendiary bombs in 1943, the church mainly retains the character of a late-Gothic hall church. The Thomanerchor, the church choir, likely founded in 1212, is an internationally known boys' choir.
Battle of the Nations Monument (Völkerschlachtdenkmal)
The Battle of the Nations Monument was built in memory of the Battle of the Nations based on designs by the Berlin architect Bruno Schmitz and inaugurated on October 18, 1913. The sculptural works were designed by the sculptors Christian Behrens and Franz Metzner. At 91 meters high, it is the highest monument in Europe and one of Leipzig's well-known landmarks. It forms a landmark with a striking silhouette that can be seen from afar and can be seen from Fichtelberg , 105 kilometers away, when the air is clear. Today it belongs to a foundation under public law of the city of Leipzig and is part of the Leipzig City History Museum . Around 300,000 people visit the monument every year. The German historian Thomas Nipperdey characterized the Battle of the Nations Monument in 1968 as a monument of “national gathering” or “national concentration”, in which the nation “sees itself as a closed national community and as a power” against the background of the victory of a coalition of nations.
